# Commissioner Guidance

## Your Role as Commissioner

As a Local Authority commissioner, you own the tariff configuration for your area. You are responsible for specifying how tariffs are applied to the providers delivering sexual health services under your commissioning arrangements.

This means you determine the tariff structure, approve rates, and ensure all participating organisations understand and operate within the agreed framework. Pathway Analytics supports you through the setup and ongoing management of your configuration, but the decisions and sign-offs sit with you.

## Configuration Ownership

The lead commissioner holds responsibility for the tariff configuration. In practice, this means:

- **Communicating changes** — You are the single point of contact for notifying providers and stakeholders of any changes to tariff arrangements
- **Approving configurations** — No configuration goes live without your explicit approval, whether it is a new setup or an in-year amendment
- **Ensuring all parties are informed** — Every participating organisation must understand the tariff structure, their obligations, and any changes that affect them
- **Maintaining accurate records** — You should keep a record of configuration decisions, approvals, and the rationale behind them

If your commissioning arrangement involves multiple Local Authorities (a cluster), the lead commissioner coordinates across all participating commissioners.

## Setup Process

Establishing a new tariff configuration follows a structured process:

1. **Initial meeting with Pathway Analytics and providers** — A kick-off meeting to agree scope, timelines, and the organisations involved. This is where you confirm the commissioning intent and identify any constraints or special requirements.

2. **Define currencies, tariff rates, and cross-charging arrangements** — Working with Pathway Analytics, you specify the activity currencies (the units of healthcare activity that will be costed), the tariff rate for each currency, and how cross-charging between commissioners and providers will operate.

3. **Set participating providers and their market forces factors or geo-weightings** — Identify all providers who will use the tariff, and confirm any geographical adjustments (market forces factors or geo-weightings) that apply to their activity.

4. **Review and approve the draft configuration** — Pathway Analytics prepares a draft configuration based on your specifications. You review this for accuracy, confirm it reflects the agreed arrangements, and provide formal approval.

5. **Go live** — Once approved, the configuration is activated in the Tariff Grouper. Providers can then submit data against the live tariff.

Allow approximately 4–6 weeks from the initial meeting to go-live, depending on the complexity of your arrangements and the number of participating organisations.

## Monitoring Responsibilities

Once your tariff is live, you have ongoing obligations to ensure it operates effectively:

- **Review tariff performance reports** — Regularly review the reports generated by the Tariff Grouper to understand activity volumes, costs, and trends across your providers
- **Ensure providers submit data within contractual deadlines** — Monitor submission compliance and follow up with providers who miss deadlines, as timely data is essential for accurate tariff calculations
- **Manage in-year changes through the governance process** — If circumstances change mid-year (new providers joining, rate adjustments, currency changes), submit these through the formal governance process rather than making ad-hoc requests
- **Coordinate annual tariff reviews** — Lead the annual review cycle to assess whether the current tariff structure remains fit for purpose, engaging providers and Pathway Analytics in the process
