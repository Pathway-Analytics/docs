# Governance

## Overview

Effective governance is essential for ensuring that tariff arrangements operate fairly, transparently, and in accordance with agreed terms across all participating organisations. This document sets out the governance framework for Sexual Health Tariff configurations managed through the Pathway Analytics Tariff Grouper, defining how decisions are made, who holds authority, and how disputes and changes are handled.

## Roles and Responsibilities

### Lead Commissioner

The Lead Commissioner holds primary accountability for the tariff configuration and its ongoing management:

- Owns the tariff configuration and acts as the authoritative point of contact for all participating organisations
- Approves changes to tariff rates, currency definitions, and configuration parameters
- Communicates updates, decisions, and policy changes to all participating commissioners and providers
- Ensures the configuration reflects current commissioning intentions and local service specifications
- Chairs governance meetings and sets the agenda for periodic reviews

### Participating Commissioners

Participating Commissioners share responsibility for local oversight within the tariff arrangement:

- Maintain local oversight of tariff application within their commissioned services
- Manage relationships with providers operating under the tariff in their area
- Escalate issues, anomalies, or disputes to the Lead Commissioner
- Participate in governance meetings and contribute to decision-making on shared matters
- Ensure local providers comply with data submission requirements

### Providers

Providers are responsible for operational compliance within the tariff framework:

- Submit activity data accurately and within agreed timescales
- Comply with data format specifications and validation requirements
- Implement tariff arrangements locally in accordance with configuration parameters
- Raise queries or concerns through the appropriate commissioner contact
- Participate in review processes when requested

### Pathway Analytics

Pathway Analytics provides technical administration and advisory support:

- Maintains the Tariff Grouper platform, ensuring system availability and data integrity
- Implements approved configuration changes as directed by the Lead Commissioner
- Provides technical advisory support on tariff structure, currency design, and data requirements
- Produces system reports and data extracts to support governance decision-making
- Escalates technical issues or data quality concerns to the Lead Commissioner

## Decision-Making Authority

### Tariff Rate Changes

Authority to approve changes to tariff rates rests with the Lead Commissioner. Rate changes require:

- A formal change request from the Lead Commissioner or a participating commissioner
- An impact assessment covering financial and operational effects on all participating organisations
- Consultation with affected providers where the change materially alters payment levels
- Written approval from the Lead Commissioner before implementation

### Adding or Removing Participating Organisations

The Lead Commissioner holds authority to add or remove organisations from the tariff arrangement:

- New organisations may be added subject to the Lead Commissioner's approval and agreement of the joining organisation to the configuration terms
- Removal of an organisation requires formal notice from either the organisation or the Lead Commissioner
- Pathway Analytics implements membership changes upon receipt of written instruction from the Lead Commissioner

### Modifying Currency Triggers

Changes to currency triggers (the activity definitions that determine tariff groupings) require:

- A documented rationale for the proposed change
- Clinical or operational justification where the change affects service categorisation
- Approval from the Lead Commissioner following consultation with affected commissioners
- An implementation timeline that allows providers to adjust data submission processes

## Escalation Procedures

### First Line: Lead Commissioner Resolution

The Lead Commissioner is the first point of contact for resolving issues:

- Operational queries from providers or participating commissioners are directed to the Lead Commissioner
- The Lead Commissioner aims to resolve matters within 10 working days
- Resolution may involve consultation with Pathway Analytics on technical matters

### Second Line: Multi-Commissioner Discussion

Where the Lead Commissioner cannot resolve an issue unilaterally, it is escalated to multi-commissioner discussion:

- The Lead Commissioner convenes a meeting of affected participating commissioners
- All parties present their position and supporting evidence
- The group seeks consensus on a resolution
- Decisions are recorded and communicated to all affected organisations

### Third Line: Formal Dispute Resolution

If multi-commissioner discussion does not reach agreement, formal dispute resolution applies:

- Either party may invoke formal dispute resolution by written notice to the Lead Commissioner
- An independent review is commissioned, drawing on the contractual terms governing the tariff arrangement
- The dispute resolution outcome is binding on all parties to the arrangement
- Pathway Analytics provides technical evidence and data as required but does not adjudicate

## Change Management

### Requesting Changes

Any participating organisation may request a change to the tariff configuration:

1. Submit a change request to the Lead Commissioner, describing the proposed change and its rationale
2. The Lead Commissioner acknowledges receipt and assigns the request for assessment
3. Requests are logged and tracked through to resolution

### Impact Assessment Requirements

All proposed changes undergo impact assessment before approval:

- Financial impact on each participating organisation (estimated cost or saving)
- Operational impact on providers (changes to data submission, reporting, or workflows)
- Technical feasibility assessment by Pathway Analytics
- Risk assessment covering unintended consequences or data quality implications

### Communication Obligations

Once a change is approved, the Lead Commissioner is responsible for communication:

- All participating commissioners receive written notification of the approved change
- Providers are notified with sufficient lead time to adjust processes
- Pathway Analytics confirms the technical implementation timeline
- Changes to data submission requirements are communicated at least 4 weeks before the effective date

### Implementation Timelines

Changes are implemented according to agreed timelines:

- Minor configuration adjustments (e.g. correcting a label or description): within 5 working days
- Rate changes or currency modifications: minimum 4 weeks' notice to all parties before the effective date
- Structural changes (adding organisations, redesigning currency groups): agreed on a case-by-case basis with a minimum of 8 weeks' notice
- Emergency changes (e.g. correcting a calculation error causing incorrect payments): implemented immediately with retrospective communication within 2 working days
