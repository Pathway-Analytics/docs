# Review Process

## Overview

Periodic reviews are essential for ensuring that tariff arrangements remain fit for purpose, reflect current service delivery patterns, and continue to represent fair value for all participating organisations. Without structured reviews, configurations risk drifting out of alignment with clinical practice, cost pressures, or commissioning intentions. This document defines the review cycle, who participates, what is assessed, and how changes are approved and implemented.

## Review Frequency

### Annual Review Cycle

A comprehensive review of each tariff configuration takes place annually, aligned with the financial year:

- The annual review window runs during Q4 (January–March) to allow approved changes to take effect from the start of the new financial year in April
- The Lead Commissioner initiates the review process by notifying all stakeholders at least 8 weeks before the intended completion date
- The review covers all aspects of the tariff configuration, including rates, currencies, participation, and data quality
- Outcomes and approved changes are documented and communicated to all parties before the new financial year begins

### Ad-Hoc Reviews

In addition to the annual cycle, ad-hoc reviews may be triggered by significant changes in circumstances:

- A new provider joining or an existing provider leaving the tariff arrangement
- A material error discovered in tariff rates, currency definitions, or cross-charging calculations
- A significant service redesign that alters the scope or nature of commissioned activity
- Changes in national policy or guidance that directly affect tariff structures
- A formal request from any participating commissioner or provider, supported by a documented rationale

Ad-hoc reviews follow the same approval workflow as annual reviews but are scoped to the specific matter that triggered them.

## Stakeholder Involvement

### Lead Commissioner

The Lead Commissioner owns and drives the review process:

- Initiates the annual review and sets the review timetable
- Chairs review meetings and sets the agenda
- Holds final approval authority for all proposed changes arising from the review
- Ensures findings and decisions are communicated to all parties

### Participating Commissioners

Participating Commissioners contribute local perspective and oversight:

- Provide feedback on tariff operation within their commissioned services
- Highlight any local issues, anomalies, or areas requiring adjustment
- Participate in review meetings and contribute to consensus on proposed changes
- Confirm that proposed changes are acceptable within their local commissioning context

### Providers

Providers contribute operational and clinical insight:

- Report on data quality issues, submission difficulties, or process inefficiencies
- Highlight any tariff categories that do not reflect current service delivery patterns
- Provide feedback on the practical impact of tariff rates on service sustainability
- Participate in review meetings when invited by the Lead Commissioner

### Pathway Analytics

Pathway Analytics provides technical and analytical support to the review process:

- Produces data reports and analysis to inform the review (activity volumes, cost trends, outlier detection, data quality metrics)
- Identifies technical issues or configuration anomalies for consideration
- Advises on the feasibility and implications of proposed changes
- Implements approved changes to the tariff configuration following the review

## Review Scope

Each annual review considers the following areas:

### Tariff Rates

- Whether current rates remain appropriate given cost inflation, service changes, and benchmarking data
- Whether rate differentials between tariff categories accurately reflect relative resource consumption
- Whether any rates require adjustment to maintain financial sustainability for providers and value for commissioners

### Currency Definitions

- Whether existing currency triggers accurately capture and categorise clinical activity
- Whether any currencies should be added, removed, or redefined to reflect changes in service delivery
- Whether coding or classification changes require corresponding currency adjustments

### Cross-Charging Arrangements

- Whether cross-charging between commissioners operates correctly and reflects actual patient flows
- Whether any disputes or anomalies in cross-charging have been identified during the year
- Whether cross-charging methodologies remain appropriate and equitable

### Provider Participation

- Whether the current list of participating providers is accurate and complete
- Whether any providers should be added to or removed from the arrangement
- Whether all providers are meeting their data submission and compliance obligations

### Data Quality

- Whether data submission completeness and timeliness meet agreed standards
- Whether validation error rates are within acceptable thresholds
- Whether any systematic data quality issues require intervention or support

## Criteria for In-Year Changes

Mid-year changes to a tariff configuration are exceptional and require clear justification. Changes outside the annual review cycle are warranted when:

- **New provider joining** — A new provider organisation begins delivering services under the tariff arrangement and requires immediate inclusion in the configuration
- **Material error discovered** — A calculation error, incorrect rate, or misconfigured currency is identified that results in incorrect payments and requires urgent correction
- **Significant service redesign** — A commissioned service undergoes substantial redesign (e.g. pathway restructuring, new clinic model) that renders current currency definitions inaccurate
- **Regulatory or policy change** — National policy changes impose requirements that necessitate immediate tariff adjustment
- **Provider departure** — A provider ceases to deliver services under the arrangement and must be removed from the configuration

In-year changes are not appropriate for matters that can reasonably wait for the annual review, such as minor rate adjustments, cosmetic configuration changes, or speculative currency additions.

## Approval Workflow

All changes arising from reviews — whether annual or ad-hoc — follow a structured approval workflow:

### Step 1: Proposal Submitted

Any stakeholder (Lead Commissioner, participating commissioner, provider, or Pathway Analytics) may submit a proposal for change:

- The proposal describes the change, its rationale, and the expected impact
- Proposals are submitted in writing to the Lead Commissioner
- The Lead Commissioner logs the proposal and acknowledges receipt

### Step 2: Impact Assessment Conducted

Pathway Analytics conducts a technical and financial impact assessment:

- Estimates the financial effect on each participating organisation
- Assesses operational implications for providers (data submission, workflows, reporting)
- Evaluates technical feasibility and identifies any implementation risks
- Documents the assessment findings in a summary report for stakeholders

### Step 3: Consultation with Affected Parties

The Lead Commissioner consults with all parties materially affected by the proposed change:

- Affected commissioners and providers are invited to comment on the impact assessment
- A consultation period of at least 10 working days is provided for non-urgent changes
- Feedback is collected and documented for consideration in the approval decision
- For urgent corrections (e.g. material errors), an expedited consultation may apply with a minimum of 3 working days

### Step 4: Lead Commissioner Approval

The Lead Commissioner makes the final approval decision:

- Reviews the impact assessment and consultation feedback
- Approves, rejects, or requests modification of the proposed change
- Documents the decision and rationale in writing
- Notifies all stakeholders of the outcome

### Step 5: Implementation by Pathway Analytics

Upon approval, Pathway Analytics implements the change:

- Applies the approved configuration changes to the Tariff Grouper
- Verifies the changes through system testing and validation
- Confirms the effective date to the Lead Commissioner
- Provides implementation confirmation to all stakeholders

### Step 6: Communication to All Parties

The Lead Commissioner ensures all parties are informed:

- Written notification of the approved change is issued to all participating commissioners and providers
- The notification includes the nature of the change, effective date, and any actions required by recipients
- Changes affecting data submission requirements are communicated at least 4 weeks before the effective date
- The updated configuration is reflected in the Tariff Grouper and available to all users from the effective date
