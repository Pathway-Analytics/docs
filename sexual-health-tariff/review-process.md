# Review Process

## Overview

London Commissioner own the London Tariff Configuration template which is widely used as the starting point for local tariff configurations.

Periodic reviews of the London template are essential for ensuring that tariff arrangements remain fit for purpose, reflect current service delivery patterns, and continue to represent fair value for all participating organisations. Without structured reviews, configurations risk drifting out of alignment with clinical practice, cost pressures, or commissioning intentions. This document defines the review cycle, who participates, what is assessed, and how changes are approved and implemented.

London Commissioners run host the annual review process and accept comments from participating organisations from beyond London.

Local Lead Commissioners are responsible for requesting any changes to their local configurations.

## London Tariff Configuration Template

### Annual Review Cycle

The annual review process follows a prescribed sequence in order to minimise unnecessary re-work.

- Review the tariff meta rules to ensure they remain fit for purpose.
- With clinical guidance, review and update pathways.  Retire pathways that are no longer needed, update or create new pathways for new clinical practice.
- Review and update staff costs from published pay scales.
- Review and update costs for consumables and drugs.
- Review and update costs for pathology.
- Review and update the pathway model currencies, create new currencies where necessary and retire those no longer needed, reallocate the underlying pathways as required.
- Reviews the currency cross-charging arrangements.
- Use a representative set of activity data that covers each currency to determine the pathway weightings and overage for each currency and apply the weightings and appropriate currency factor.
- Update tariff rates for currency with fixed or predetermined tariff rates.
- Publish a tentative draft tariff configuration.
- Compile a financial impact assessment from a representative set of activity data being parsed through the draft tariff configuration (and include the appropriate MFFs) and compare the results to the actual charges raised by that activity.
- London Commissioners agree a new London template tariff configuration for publication.

The current London template tariff configuration is the configuration we recommend commissioners adopt when they have the opportunity to refresh their contractual arrangements.

Commissioners may elect to adopt any part of the London template tariff configuration and apply their own tariff rates as they wish.  Commissioners may want to consider applying a global factor to the tariff configuration to bring the total charges into line with their budgetary constraints or negotiated contractual position.

### Ad-Hoc Reviews

In addition to the annual cycle, ad-hoc reviews may be triggered by significant changes in circumstances:

- A new provider joining or an existing provider leaving the tariff arrangement.
- A material error discovered in tariff rates, currency definitions, or cross-charging calculations.
- A significant service redesign that alters the scope or nature of commissioned activity.
- Changes in clinical practice such as the introduction of a new screening regime.
- Changes in national policy or guidance that directly affect tariff structures.

Ad-hoc reviews follow the same approval workflow as annual reviews but are scoped to the specific matter that triggered them.

## Local Tariff Configuration Reviews

### Lead Commissioner

A Lead Commissioner may initiate a review of their locally hosted tariff configuration whenever the need arises.

We would recommend local tariff configurations are reviewed annually.  If changes are required it is easier to implement at them at the beginning of the financial year.

### Ad Hoc Updates 

Updates can be quickly implemented, major changes may warrant a name change in the configuration to make it clear in the charging there has been a change.

If ad hoc changes are required in-year, the Lead Commissioner needs to state the date the change is to be effective from and if this is a prior date, they must state if any prior submissions should be re-processed to adopt the new arrangement. 

#### Criteria for In-Year Changes

In-year changes to a tariff configuration are exceptional and require clear justification. Changes outside the annual review cycle are warranted when:

- **New provider joining** — A new provider organisation begins delivering services under the tariff arrangement and requires immediate inclusion in the configuration
- **Material error discovered** — A calculation error, incorrect rate, or misconfigured currency is identified that results in incorrect payments and requires urgent correction
- **Significant service redesign** — A commissioned service undergoes substantial redesign (e.g. pathway restructuring, new clinic model) that renders current currency definitions inaccurate
- **Change in Clinical Practice/Guidance** — New guidance indicates a specific clinical activity should come under a tariff arrangement.
- **Provider departure** — A provider ceases to deliver services under the arrangement and must be removed from the configuration

## Pathway Analytics Role

Pathway Analytics provides technical and analytical support to the review process:

- Produces data reports and analysis to inform the review (activity volumes, cost trends, outlier detection, data quality metrics)
- Supports the price capture activity.
- Identifies technical issues or configuration anomalies for consideration.
- Advises on the feasibility and implications of proposed changes.
- Implements approved changes to the tariff configuration following the review.
